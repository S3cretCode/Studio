ChargedUP – Behind the Scenes of an RC Car

Unveiling the scientific synergy that powers play

Discover the intricate dance between chemistry and physics that brings a simple RC toy car to life. From the hidden chemical reactions within its batteries to the magnetic forces that spin its motor, we explore the real-world science behind everyday technology.

Explore Chemistry Explore Physics

Project Context & Learning Objectives

This Grade 11 (Manitoba) Chemistry 30S and Physics 30S final project dissects the mechanisms of a battery-powered RC car, serving as a concrete case study to illuminate core curriculum concepts. Our goal is to demonstrate a deep, interdisciplinary understanding, meeting high academic standards while maintaining clarity and specificity.

What you'll learn:

Battery Chemistry (Chem 30S)

Understand how AA alkaline battery chemistry (Zn / MnO₂) governs available charge and energy through stoichiometry, limiting reactant analysis, and electron transfer.

Electrical & Magnetic Principles (Physics 30S)

Explore how chemical energy transforms into electric current, and how moving charge produces magnetic fields, enabling motion within a DC motor.

Interdisciplinary Connection

Grasp the direct chain from chemical stoichiometry to electrical output, magnetic fields, and ultimately, mechanical motion, emphasizing how chemistry constrains physics.
