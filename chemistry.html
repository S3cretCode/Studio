The Chemical Heart: AA Alkaline Batteries

Reaction & Stoichiometry

The standard AA battery relies on the redox reaction between Zinc and Manganese Dioxide. The simplified net reaction is:

$$Zn(s) + 2MnO_2(s) + H_2O(l) \rightarrow ZnO(s) + 2MnO(OH)(s)$$

Oxidation (Anode): $$Zn \rightarrow Zn^{2+} + 2e^-$$ (1 mol Zn produces 2 mol electrons)

Reduction (Cathode): $$MnO_2 + e^- \rightarrow MnO(OH)$$ (1 mol MnO₂ consumes 1 mol electrons)

Optional Extension: NiMH

Nickel-Metal Hydride batteries use a different reaction ($MH + NiO(OH) \rightarrow M + Ni(OH)_2$) allowing for recharging and lower internal resistance.
